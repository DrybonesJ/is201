Writing Resources

Most of my writing philosophy came through the discovery of the many resources offered by Brandon Sanderson. I first became comfortable writing my first attempt at a novel after finding Brandon’s lecture series taught at BYU. This page will compile some of those resources into one.

Feel free to explore these sources. I've found them to be incredibly helpful in helping me to develop my craft. Hopefully they can be of great use to you as well. Good luck!

Writing Excuses

Writing Excuses is a podcast that features four professional writers in covering an immense, in-depth discussion of all the different components of writing. On the panel are Brandon Sanderson, Dan Wells, Howard Taylor, and Mary Robinette Kowal. Together, they represent a variety of genres and types of fiction, so their collective brain provides a vast sum of knowledge on the subject of writing.

This is probably the single best resource for writing that I know about. Each episode is relatively short—around 15 minutes—allowing for you to quickly catch an episode during a busy day. There are now hundreds of episodes which, all together, account for the equivalent of a university course. Arguably better.

I highly recommend. The link to their website is listed below. You can listen for free.

Brandon Sanderson Lecture Series

This lecture series was my first exposure to all the complexities of writing. I think that Brandon does a fantastic job breaking down the most essential components of writing. This class is taught at BYU and represents a semester class. Graciously, Brandon has posted these on YouTube so that we can enjoy them for free.
